\chapter{Problem Statement}

%In recent years, many digital tools have been developed to support arithmetic
%learning in primary education. Platforms such as \textit{Automatus},
%\textit{Rekentuin}, and \textit{Prodigy Math} have shown that adaptive practice
%and gamification can increase motivation and improve arithmetic fluency. At the
%same time, researchers studying arithmetic learning rely on controlled experiment
%software such as OpenSesame \cite{mathot_opensesame_2012} to run their studies.
%
%The problem is that these two worlds do not connect well. Classroom tools are not
%built for research: they offer little control over experimental conditions and do
%not collect the data that researchers need. Research tools, on the
%other hand, are not built for classrooms: they require technical knowledge to set
%up, look unappealing, and have no gamification or story. This makes it hard to
%keep children motivated across multiple sessions, which directly affects the
%quality of the research.
%
%This matters because educational research depends on ecological validity. A child
%who loses motivation after two sessions, or who is distracted by an unfamiliar and
%ugly interface, is not a reliable research participant. Right now, researchers are
%forced to choose between methodological control and motivational design, even
%though both are necessary.
%
%\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=red!5!white, colframe=red!60!black, title=\textbf{Problem}]
%Existing digital arithmetic tools serve either classroom practice or controlled
%research, but not both. Classroom tools lack the configurability and data
%collection needed for research. Research tools lack the motivational design
%needed to keep children engaged across multiple sessions. This forces
%researchers to compromise on either scientific rigour or ecological validity.
%\end{tcolorbox}
%
%\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!4!white, colframe=blue!40!black, title=\textbf{Goal}]
%\textbf{Design, develop, and evaluate a web-based arithmetic learning platform
%that works for both classroom use and educational research.}
%The platform, RekenRangers, combines a gamified and child-friendly environment
%with the configurability needed for controlled experiments. Features such as
%metacognitive self-assessment prompts, adaptive difficulty, timer settings, and
%structured exercise sequences can all be configured by a teacher or researcher
%without any programming knowledge. The tool is evaluated in two ways:
%quantitatively, by replicating an existing study on metacognition in arithmetic
%to check whether the tool produces results consistent with controlled research
%software; and qualitatively, by surveying teachers, researchers, and students
%on the usability and motivation the platform offers.
%\end{tcolorbox}
%
%RekenRangers is not meant to replace existing classroom tools or research
%software. The goal is to fill the gap between them --- a platform that is
%both engaging enough to keep children motivated, and flexible enough to support
%meaningful research.
